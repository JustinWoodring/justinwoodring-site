This is what's actually on the desk. The page exists because I'd rather you judge the work than the slide deck — but the tools are part of the work.

## Daily driver

- **Coding agent:** **pi**. The primary way I get software built these days.
- **Editor:** **Neovim** for almost everything. **Zed** when I want a real GUI. **nano** for the inevitable moment a terminal's `TERM` is broken.
- **Terminal:** **Kitty**, on every machine that runs it.
- **Shell:** **Bash** with **Starship** as the prompt. Not pretty for pretty's sake; pretty for legibility's sake.
- **OS:** **macOS** on a MacBook Pro for daily work. **Windows PC dualbooting Arch Linux** when I want the literal metal and POSIX semantics for the things macOS gets in the way of.

## Languages, by affection

1. **Rust** — the language I reach for when something has to last. Memory model, type system, ecosystem. Suitable for most things.
2. **Scala** — quietly elegant. I keep coming back to the dual-paradigm clarity.
3. **Ruby** — the language that taught me programmer happiness is a feature.
4. **PHP** — underappreciated, more serious than its reputation, and it pays a lot of the bills on the open web.
5. **C** — when the system demands it. Rarer than people think.
6. **Python** — I don't love it, but I'm fluent. I use it for AI things where the data is the point and the ecosystem is non-negotiable.
7. **SolidJS** over React. The reactivity model is closer to what I want; the bundle is honest about its size.

## What I run

- **Slatron** — distributed signage + AI DJ orchestration, Rust, Rhai scripting, local ONNX-based TTS.
- **Lorefire** — local-first transcription + LLM pipeline. Encrypted. Sovereign. Mine.
- **Capibara** — Rust tooling for C ecosystem documentation.
- **Qubit Simulator** — three.js Bloch-sphere visualizer for teaching.
- **AutopilotV** — a desktop orchestrator that runs the coding-agent CLIs (including pi itself) through the full PR-review and dev lifecycle. Deterministically booked, judgment-assisted, human-gated.
- **aipolctl** — a Rust implementation of the *Policed Agency* architecture: capability-gated IAM for AI agents, multi-tier escalation, asymmetric trust protocols. The policy engine that lets agents act like employees instead of root.

## Influence

- **Judo and Iaido.** Two different shapes of the same discipline. Judo: yield to force, redirect it, end the contest efficiently — leverage over strength. Iaido: the cut happens once, decisively, in the space where hesitation would have lived. The way I want to write software is the way I want to move in either dojo.
- **Isaac Asimov.** Foundation for what it says about humanity and empire at civilizational scale. The Robot stories and Three Laws for what they say about autonomous agents and the engineering of ethics into systems that have to keep humans accountable. Both halves matter; the second half is why my research reads the way it does.

## What I've stopped using

- Heavyweight IDEs. The terminal is the IDE.
- Browser-based AI assistants as a default. Useful in narrow cases; corrupting as a habit.
- Notifications. Most of them are an attack on attention.
